About Beagles

The Beagle is a breed of small scent hound, similar in appearance to the much larger foxhound. The beagle was developed primarily for hunting hare, known as beagling. Possessing a great sense of smell and superior tracking instincts, the beagle is the primary breed used as a detection dog for prohibited agricultural imports and foodstuffs in quarantine around the world.

The beagle is intelligent and is a popular pet due to its size, good temper, and a lack of inherited health problems. These friendly, curious dogs are merry and outgoing. They enjoy company and are generally good with other dogs and children.

Beagle Trivia

  1. Origin: Beagles originated in England as hunting dogs.
  2. Size: They typically stand between 13 and 15 inches at the shoulder.
  3. Temperament: Beagles are friendly, curious, and merry.
  4. Sense of Smell: Beagles have approximately 220 million scent receptors, making them one of the best breeds for scent detection.
  5. Famous Beagle: Snoopy from the Peanuts comics is the world's most famous beagle.
  6. Vocalization: Beagles have a distinctive bay or howl when they catch a scent.
  7. Lifespan: The average lifespan of a beagle is 12-15 years.
  8. Exercise Needs: They require regular exercise to prevent boredom and obesity.
